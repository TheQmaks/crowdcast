# City Council Reverses Zoning Decision After Public Outcry

**March 15, 2026** — The Millbrook City Council voted 4-3 to reverse its controversial zoning decision that would have allowed industrial development in the Riverside residential district.

Mayor Patricia Chen, who originally championed the rezoning proposal, issued a brief statement calling the reversal "premature" and warning of economic consequences. Council member David Park, who led the opposition, called it "a victory for the residents of Riverside."

The reversal comes after two weeks of intense public pressure. Local journalist Maria Santos published a series of investigative articles revealing that the original zoning proposal had been fast-tracked without the required environmental impact assessment. Her reporting was shared widely on social media, sparking outrage among residents.

The Riverside Residents Association, led by retired teacher Robert Kim, organized three protests attracting over 500 people each. University students from nearby Millbrook State joined the protests, with the student government passing a resolution condemning the rezoning.

However, the Millbrook Chamber of Commerce, represented by its president Angela Torres, warned that the reversal would cost the city an estimated $12 million in development revenue and deter future investment.

Local business owners remain divided. Small business owners in Riverside largely support the reversal, fearing industrial traffic would hurt their businesses. Meanwhile, construction and logistics companies had been counting on the new industrial zone for growth.

Environmental group GreenMillbrook praised the decision but cautioned that the underlying environmental concerns — aging infrastructure and flood risk in Riverside — still need to be addressed.

The debate continues on social media, with hashtags #SaveRiverside and #MillbrookJobs trending locally. Political analysts suggest the issue could become central to the upcoming mayoral election in November.
